# Example: AI-Sector Portfolio

## Asset

AI-sector long book: high-quality AI infrastructure, data-center power chain, and selected enterprise AI application companies.

## Core Thesis

AI adoption creates durable demand for compute, data-center infrastructure, power infrastructure, and workflow automation.

## Wanted Exposures

- AI compute demand growth
- data-center capex expansion
- enterprise AI production adoption
- power infrastructure demand

## Unwanted Exposures

- broad Nasdaq beta
- rate sensitivity
- AI regulation shock
- Taiwan semiconductor supply-chain risk
- chip export-control escalation
- AI capex slowdown
- model commoditization

## Candidate Semantic Hedges

### AI Regulation

Bad state:

Strict AI regulation reduces deployment speed or valuation multiples.

Candidate hedge:

Prediction-market contracts on AI regulation, model restrictions, safety-law passage, or major enforcement actions.

Key basis risk:

The contract may settle on a narrow legal event while portfolio losses come from broader regulatory uncertainty.

### Taiwan Supply-Chain Risk

Bad state:

Taiwan conflict or blockade risk harms semiconductor supply chains.

Candidate hedge:

Prediction-market contracts on Taiwan conflict escalation or semiconductor disruption, plus traditional supply-chain hedges.

Key basis risk:

Portfolio drawdown can occur from risk repricing before the prediction market resolves.

### Model Commoditization

Bad state:

Open-source or low-cost models compress margins for closed-model API providers.

Candidate hedge:

Contracts on benchmark milestones, open-source model releases, or product adoption events where available.

Key basis risk:

Benchmarks may not translate into enterprise pricing pressure.

## Evaluation Question

For every candidate hedge:

```text
Would this hedge make money in the exact scenario where this AI portfolio loses money?
```
